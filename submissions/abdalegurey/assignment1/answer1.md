### 1:
-Data Science is the field of collecting, cleaning, analyzing, and interpreting data to discover useful insights and support decision-making. It combines statistics, programming, and domain knowledge to understand data.

-Machine Learning (ML) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that enables computers to learn patterns from data and make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed for every task.

-Relationship Between Data Science and Machine Learning

Data Science and Machine Learning are closely related:

Data Science focuses on understanding and preparing data, finding patterns, and generating insights.
Machine Learning uses that prepared data to build models that can predict future outcomes or automate decisions

### 5:

Training data is used to train the Machine Learning model, while test data is used to evaluate how well the model performs on new, unseen data.

A dataset is commonly split into:

80% Training Data
20% Test Data

Why is this necessary?

To measure the model's real performance.
To detect overfitting.
To ensure the model can generalize to new data.

Example: If you have 1,000 records, 800 may be used for training and 200 for testing. The model learns from the 800 records and is evaluated using the 200 unseen records


### 6:


Researchers used Machine Learning to help detect breast cancer from medical imaging data. They collected patient data, cleaned and prepared it, and then trained a Machine Learning model to identify whether a tumor was cancerous or not.

# Findings:
The study found that the Machine Learning model achieved high accuracy in detecting breast cancer, helping doctors make faster and more reliable diagnoses. The model reduced the chances of human error and improved early detection rates.

 # Data Science Lifecycle Stages Covered:

Problem Definition – Detect breast cancer accurately.
Data Collection – Gather patient and medical imaging data.
Data Cleaning & Preparation – Remove errors and prepare the data.
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) – Analyze patterns in the data.
Model Building (Machine Learning) – Train the model to detect cancer.
Model Evaluation – Measure accuracy and performance.
Deployment – Use the model to assist healthcare professionals.
